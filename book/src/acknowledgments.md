# Acknowledgments

This book wouldn't exist without the giants whose shoulders I stand on.

**Robert Nystrom**'s *Crafting Interpreters* showed me that technical books could be both rigorous and readable, pedagogical without being patronizing. His approach of building something real while teaching the concepts inspired the structure of this book.


The **OSDev Wiki** community has created an invaluable resource for aspiring kernel developers. 
**Intel's Software Developer Manuals** remain the authoritative source for x86_64 architecture details, even if they're not always the most approachable reading.

Thanks to the broader systems programming community on forums, IRC, and Stack Overflow who've answered countless "why doesn't my bootloader work?" questions over the years.

Finally, thank you for reading this book. I hope it provide you with the required knowledge to understand the fundamentals of an operating system and enables you to extend the functionality further on your own.
