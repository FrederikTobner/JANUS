# Acknowledgments

This book wouldn't exist without the giants whose shoulders I stand on.

## Inspiration

**Robert Nystrom**'s *Crafting Interpreters* showed me that technical books could be both rigorous and readable, pedagogical without being patronizing. His approach of building something real while teaching the concepts inspired the structure of this book.

**Oscar Toledo G.**'s *Programming Boot Sector Games* proved that you can teach low-level programming with creativity and joy. His ability to make bare-metal x86 assembly approachable and fun reminded me that even the most technical topics benefit from a human touch.

## Technical Resources

The **OSDev Wiki** community has created an invaluable resource for aspiring kernel developers. Countless late-night debugging sessions were saved by their documentation.

**Intel's Software Developer Manuals** remain the authoritative source for x86_64 architecture details, even if they're not always the most approachable reading.

## Tools

This project relies heavily on open-source tools:
- **CMake** for build management
- **QEMU** for testing without risking real hardware
- **GDB** for debugging the impossible
- **mdBook** for creating this book itself

## Community

Thanks to the broader systems programming community on forums, IRC, and Stack Overflow who've answered countless "why doesn't my bootloader work?" questions over the years.

## You

Finally, thank you for reading this book. Building an OS is a journey best taken with companions. I hope you find it as educational and rewarding as I have.
